# Content Guide — Sat Apps Marketplace

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the writing style and content tone for Sat Apps Marketplace.

It should help all screen copy feel:
- premium
- clear
- modern
- business-focused
- trustworthy
- conversion-oriented

The goal is to make the product feel polished not only visually, but also in language.

---

## 2. Brand Voice

Sat Apps should sound like:

- premium
- confident
- clear
- modern
- solution-driven
- trustworthy
- business-ready

It should not sound like:

- overly casual
- playful
- noisy
- hype-heavy
- too technical
- robotic
- generic sales spam

---

## 3. Tone Rules

### Use a tone that is:
- clean
- concise
- polished
- professional
- calm
- persuasive without being aggressive

### Avoid a tone that is:
- loud
- overly promotional
- repetitive
- vague
- too wordy
- too corporate and cold

---

## 4. Writing Principles

## A. Clarity first
Every section should be understandable quickly.

## B. Premium brevity
Shorter, better copy feels more premium than long paragraphs.

## C. Business value language
Focus on:
- outcomes
- product quality
- customization
- scalability
- trust
- clarity

## D. Visual readability
Write for screen reading, not essay reading.

Use:
- short sections
- short paragraphs
- clean headline structure

---

## 5. Headline Style

Headlines should feel:
- bold
- polished
- direct
- slightly aspirational

### Good examples
- Build premium digital products
- Find the right app for your business
- Designed to sell better
- Built for strong business presentation
- Let’s discuss your next digital product

### Avoid
- Welcome to our amazing website
- Best solutions for all your needs
- We provide many services for everyone
- The most innovative app platform ever

---

## 6. Subheadline Style

Subheadlines should:
- support the title
- explain value
- stay readable
- avoid too much detail

### Good examples
- Browse solutions by category, sort products quickly, and save the ones you want to revisit.
- Present your products with stronger branding, cleaner layout, and a polished marketplace experience.
- Share your idea, request a demo, or ask for a custom build.

### Rule
Keep them:
- informative
- calm
- not too long

---

## 7. CTA Writing Style

CTA text should be:
- short
- action-led
- confident

### Preferred CTA examples
- Explore Apps
- Contact Us
- Browse Apps
- Start Project
- Request Demo
- View Details
- Submit Inquiry

### Avoid
- Click Here
- Learn More Now
- Get Started Today Right Away
- Submit Form Immediately

---

## 8. Eyebrow / Label Style

Eyebrow text should be:
- short
- uppercase
- category-like
- premium and structured

### Examples
- PREMIUM APP MARKETPLACE
- FEATURED APPS
- OVERVIEW
- FEATURE HIGHLIGHTS
- NEXT STEP
- START YOUR PROJECT
- OUR STORY
- OUR VALUES

### Rule
Use eyebrows to organize, not decorate excessively.

---

## 9. Product Copy Style

When describing apps, the copy should focus on:

- premium quality
- business use case
- modular structure
- customization potential
- practical value

### Good product copy themes
- premium business suite
- scalable digital solution
- customizable product flow
- clean product presentation
- admin-ready structure
- web and mobile ready

### Avoid
- too much technical implementation detail
- feature dumping without hierarchy
- overpromising unrealistic outcomes

---

## 10. About Page Tone

The About screen should sound:
- confident
- thoughtful
- trustworthy
- quality-driven

### Focus areas
- who the company is
- what it believes in
- why it builds products this way
- how it supports businesses

### Avoid
- sounding too self-promotional
- long company-history style storytelling
- generic “we are passionate” filler

---

## 11. Contact Page Tone

The Contact screen should sound:
- inviting
- simple
- supportive
- low-friction

### Focus areas
- starting the conversation
- making inquiry feel easy
- showing professionalism
- keeping instructions clear

### Avoid
- overly formal/legal language
- too much pressure
- too many words around the form

---

## 12. Footer Content Tone

Footer copy should feel:
- calm
- elegant
- useful
- structured

### Brand line example
- Premium marketplace for modern business applications, digital products, and scalable custom solutions.

### Footer links
Keep link text:
- clear
- short
- standard
- not creative for the sake of it

Examples:
- Home
- Marketplace
- Contact Us
- Request Demo
- Custom Project
- Sales Inquiry

---

## 13. Sentence Style

Preferred sentence style:
- medium-short
- clean
- readable
- polished

### Good pattern
- We design premium digital products for modern businesses.
- Browse solutions built for presentation, usability, and scale.
- Contact us to discuss customization, pricing, and demo availability.

### Avoid
- very long multi-clause sentences
- excessive commas
- repetitive sentence openings
- filler adjectives

---

## 14. Word Choice Rules

Prefer words like:
- premium
- polished
- scalable
- modern
- tailored
- business-ready
- structured
- customizable
- refined
- efficient

Avoid overusing:
- amazing
- incredible
- revolutionary
- world-class
- ultimate
- cutting-edge
- unmatched

These often make premium brands sound less credible.

---

## 15. Content Density Rules

### Keep sections balanced
- Hero: short
- Overview: medium
- Features: structured
- CTA: short
- About story: medium
- Contact form support text: short

### Rule
If a section feels heavy, reduce text before adding more design.

Premium usually means:
- fewer words
- stronger meaning
- better pacing

---

## 16. Repetition Rules

Avoid repeating the same phrases too often, especially:
- premium
- modern
- scalable
- business-ready
- custom

These are good words, but they should be rotated with supporting language.

Use variation such as:
- polished
- refined
- tailored
- structured
- built for growth
- designed for stronger presentation

---

- strong first impression

## Apps

- discovery-focused

## AppDetails

- trust-building

## About
Tone:
- thoughtful
- brand-driven
- credible

## Contact
Tone:
- supportive
- easy
- professional

---

## 18. Content Quality Checklist

Before finalizing copy, check:

- Is it clear on first read?
- Is it shorter than it could be?
- Does it sound premium?
- Does it sound trustworthy?
- Does it help the user decide what to do next?
- Does it avoid sounding generic?

If yes, the copy is likely strong.

---

## 19. Final Standard

All writing in Sat Apps Marketplace should feel like:

- a premium digital product brand
- a trusted business partner
- a polished marketplace experience
- a brand that values clarity and quality

The content should support the design, not compete with it.
